**Section 1: Understanding Anxiety (Clinical) (1:30-4:00):**

Anxiety isn't just worry. It's your nervous system stuck in threat mode.

Here's what happens:
- Your **amygdala** (fear center) detects a threat
- It triggers **cortisol and adrenaline** (stress hormones)
- Your body prepares to fight, flee, or freeze
- Your rational brain (prefrontal cortex) goes offline
- You can't "think your way out" because your body is in survival mode

This is why people with anxiety have physical symptoms:
- Racing heart
- Shallow breathing
- Muscle tension
- Digestive issues
- Brain fog

**This isn't a lack of faith. It's biology.**

And the good news? God designed your nervous system to calm down—when you give it the right tools.

**Section 3: Practical Tools (6:30-9:30):**

So how do you actually calm anxiety? Here are two tools:

**Tool 1: 4-7-8 Breathing (activates calm)**
1. Breathe in through your nose for 4 counts
2. Hold for 7 counts
3. Exhale slowly through your mouth for 8 counts
4. Repeat 4 times

Why this works: Long exhales activate your vagus nerve, which signals safety to your brain.

**Tool 2: Scripture Meditation (rewires your mind)**
Pick one verse (like Isaiah 41:10)
1. Read it slowly 3 times
2. Close your eyes and repeat it silently
3. Visualize God speaking it to you
4. Notice where you feel tension in your body and breathe into that space

This isn't just "positive thinking"—it's biblical meditation that rewires neural pathways.
